Woman shot during drug deal gone bad in Auburn parking lot

by SHAWN SKAGER, Auburn Reporter Sports Reporter

May 9, 2014 at 2:00PM

A woman was shot during a drug deal turned robbery Friday morning in Auburn.

According to Auburn Police, the victim, a woman in her mid-30s, met with two suspects – a white man in his mid-20s and a black woman in her mid-30s – in a vehicle in a parking lot in the 1400 block of Auburn Way South at 9:40 a.m. for a drug transaction.

After the male suspect allegedly pulled a handgun, a fight broke out in the car. The man then exited the car and allegedly fired an unknown number of rounds at the victim, who was sitting in the driver's seat.

The victim was hit in the neck and transported to Harborview Medical Center in Seattle. Auburn Police did not have any information about the woman's condition.

Both suspects attempted to flee the scene but were apprehended nearby by Auburn Police, who continue to investigate the case.

HCC, coalition host forum on human trafficking May 20

May 12, 2014 at 3:00PM
For the Reporter

Highline Community College Women's Programs – in partnership with the Southwest King County Coalition Against Trafficking (SWKCAT) – hosts "Her Choice, Our Choice!," a forum to raise awareness about human trafficking.

The program is from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m. Tuesday, May 20 at the Highline Community College Student Union Building, No. 8, Mount Constance Room.

Learn more about the issue from two perspectives, the victim and the buyer, and how the community can end sex trafficking.

Lunch will be provided.

HCC Women's Programs, SWKCAT, Federal Way Coalition Against Trafficking and Washington Engage are sponsoring the event.

The forum is free.
